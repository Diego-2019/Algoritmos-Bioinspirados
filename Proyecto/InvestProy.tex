\begin{abstract}    
    %! Llenar con el abstract
    Graph coloring is a fundamental problem in graph theory and combinatorial optimization,
    where the goal is to assign colors to the vertices of a graph such that no two adjacent 
    vertices share the same color. This problem has many applications in real-world problems
    such as scheduling, register allocation, and frequency assignment. Despite its simplicity
    in definition, the problem is NP-hard, making it computationally challenging for large and complex 
    graphs.
\end{abstract}
